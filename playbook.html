Introduction

Welcome to Checkr Sales

This playbook is your handbook for your first year. Read it, use it, refer back to it.

Congratulations on joining the Checkr Sales team. You're stepping into a role on one of the fastest-moving companies in the background screening space — a market where the incumbents are legacy, the product is genuinely better, and the opportunity to build a meaningful book of business is real.

This playbook is designed to answer the question every new AE asks: "What do I actually need to do, know, and prove in my first year?" It covers your onboarding structure, how we sell, your daily activity minimums, how to use your tools, what your milestones are, and where to find everything else.

Sales Methodology

How We Sell at Checkr

The frameworks and methodology every AE is expected to operate from day one.

Checkr uses three interconnected frameworks. You'll learn all three in pre-work and practice them in bootcamp — but you need to know them before you touch a live account.

01
Command of the Message (CoM)
Before / After Scenario, Required Capabilities, How We Do It Better, Proof Points, Discovery Questions. Every conversation starts here.
02
MEDDPICC
Metrics · Economic Buyer · Decision Criteria · Decision Process · Identify Pain · Champion · Competition. Applied to qualify and advance every opp.
03
Checkr Sales Process — 7 Stages
Prospect → Qualify → Discovery → Demo → Pricing → Legal/Security → Closed Won. Each stage has defined entry/exit criteria in Salesforce.
04
Displacement Sales Model
Most prospects already have a screening vendor. Your job is to articulate the cost of inertia and the risk of staying on a legacy platform.
🎯
Core Selling Principle
Pain before product. Always.
We do not open calls by talking about Checkr. We open by understanding the prospect's world — their current screening vendor, their frustrations, their compliance exposure, and what a better solution would mean for their business. Product comes after pain is confirmed.

Your Onboarding Structure

Three Phases, One Year

Onboarding is not a two-week sprint. It's a structured year-long journey with defined checkpoints.

Phase 1
Pre-Work
Days 1–5 of employment · ~22 hours total · Must complete before Bootcamp
  • Day 1: HR, IT, Manager 1:1 (~4 hrs)
  • Day 2: Welcome, Tools Setup, The Problem We Solve (~5 hrs)
  • Day 3: Product 101 — full product suite (~5 hrs)
  • Day 4: ICP, CoM, MEDDPICC, Sales Process (~4 hrs)
  • Day 5: Competitors, Call Overviews, Outbound review (~4.5 hrs)
Phase 2
Bootcamp
5 days · In-person · San Francisco or Denver · 9 AM–5 PM
  • Day 1: Context & Foundation — pains, personas, competition
  • Day 2: Practicing Conversations — cold call & discovery role-plays
  • Day 3: How We Sell — tools, demo & pricing role-plays
  • Day 4: Outbound Motion — research, messaging, sequencing
  • Day 5: Productivity — tools, deal mechanics, certifications
Phase 3
Post-Bootcamp
Week 1 through Year 1 · Coaching + modules + milestones
  • Live outreach begins Week 3
  • Advanced industry deep-dives (Weeks 3–4)
  • Weekly Gong call reviews with manager
  • Bi-weekly Homeroom modules (ongoing)
  • Milestone check-ins at 30, 45, 90, 180, 365 days
1
Days 1–5 · Pre-Work
Complete all 10 Mindtickle modules before Bootcamp
Self-paced modules covering product, ICP, CoM, MEDDPICC, competitors, and all 4 call types. Non-negotiable. Bootcamp Day 1 assumes all pre-work is done.

Cert Rubric — What You're Scored On
  • Cold Call: Hook, value bridge, objection handling, clear CTA
  • Discovery: MAP framework, question quality, pain surfacing, CoM alignment
  • Demo: Discovery-to-demo bridge, product relevance, storytelling
  • Pricing: Lead with value, objection handling, packaging, close attempt
  • Written Product: Packages, screening types, ICP, CoM — open book format
Retake Policy
  • All certs scored immediately after submission in Mindtickle
  • Your manager sees scores in real time
  • Failed cert → retake within 5 business days
  • Manager is required to coach before any retake
  • Enablement monitors retake completion via Mindtickle
  • Retake results are tracked separately in program reporting

💡
Pro Tip — Calendar Blocking
Protect your prospecting time first
Block 9–11 AM every day for outbound calls before anything else lands on your calendar. The best call pickup rates are 8–10 AM and 4–5 PM in the prospect's timezone. Your manager can show you their calendar template on your first 1:1.

Outbound Motion

How Outbound Works at Checkr

A quick overview — the full detail lives in the Outbound Playbook.

Checkr AEs own their own pipeline. Inbound helps, but it won't fill your number. The Outbound Playbook covers everything in depth — this section gives you the framework.

The 5 Outbound Channels
  • Phone: Primary channel. Best times: 8–10 AM and 4–5 PM in prospect TZ
  • Email via Outreach: Personalized sequences — not mass blast
  • LinkedIn: Research + InMail + connection requests
  • Events: 6 event types — pre/post event sequences in Outreach
  • Partners: ATS referrals, partner team introductions
Account Prioritization Order
  • Tier 1: High intent (Demandbase MQA) in ICP industries
  • Tier 2: UserGems job-change signals — former champion moved to new co
  • Tier 3: Closed-lost re-engagement (review with manager)
  • Tier 4: ICP-fit companies with no prior Checkr history
  • Unassigned: Check Salesforce views for unowned accounts in territory
