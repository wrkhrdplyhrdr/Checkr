Outbound Philosophy

Pipeline is Your Responsibility

Inbound exists. It is not a strategy.

Every AE at Checkr owns their own pipeline. Inbound leads supplement your business — they do not replace the work of building it. The reps who hit quota in year one are the ones who treated outbound as a professional practice, not a necessary inconvenience.

Checkr is a displacement sale. The vast majority of your prospects already have a background screening vendor. Your job is not to sell them something new — it is to show them that their current vendor is costing them more than they realize, in time, compliance exposure, candidate drop-off, and operational complexity.

Target Accounts

ICP & Personas

Who you're targeting and why — the five industries Checkr wins in, and the four buyer personas.

Checkr's ICP spans five core industries where background screening volume is high, compliance requirements are complex, and the cost of a bad hire is significant. Your territory will be mapped to a subset of these industries — know the pain points cold before you pick up the phone.

🛍

Best persona: VP HR, TA Director

Best persona: CHRO, Regional HR Director
🚗

Best persona: Head of Operations, CPO
🏥

Best persona: CHRO, Compliance Officer
💻
Tech / SaaS
150–5,000 employees
Pain: ATS integration, international hiring, candidate experience
Best persona: VP People, Head of TA
The 4 Buyer Personas
  • CHRO / VP People: Strategic buyer. Cares about employer brand, compliance risk, and board-level metrics. Not in the weeds of screening ops.
  • VP / Director of TA: Cares about time-to-fill, candidate conversion rates, and ATS integration reliability.
  • HR Operations / People Ops: Your champion. Closest to the operational pain. Runs the screening workflow day-to-day.
  • CPO / Head of Product (Gig/Tech): Cares about API reliability, developer experience, and SLA guarantees.
Signals an Account is Worth Pursuing
  • High intent score in Demandbase (MQA status)
  • Recent job posting for HR Ops, TA, or Compliance roles
  • Company is growing headcount rapidly (LinkedIn org signals)
  • Former Checkr champion moved to this company (UserGems)
  • Industry-specific regulatory change creating compliance urgency
  • Current vendor is First Advantage, Sterling, or HireRight
  • Closed-lost within last 12 months — revisit with new angle

Outreach / Athean

Sequencing Strategy

How to use Outreach sequences correctly — from account entry through touch completion.

There are 45+ sequences in Outreach tagged and accessible via Athean. Do not create your own sequences before reviewing these with your manager. The Commercial Collection is your default starting point for most accounts.

⚠️
Before You Start
Confirm sequence access and account approval with your manager first
Do not add accounts to sequences until: (1) your account list is approved by your manager, (2) you've confirmed the account isn't already owned or in an active deal, and (3) you've done the 5-step research workflow. Un-sequencing a contact mid-touch creates a bad experience. Do the prep first.

Email & LinkedIn

Messaging That Gets Responses

The principles and templates that drive reply rates. Personalize every single one.

What Gets Replies
  • Specificity: Reference the prospect's company, industry, or recent news
  • Pain-first openers: Start with their problem, not your product
  • Short: Under 100 words for first email. Under 80 for follow-ups.
  • One CTA: "Are you open to a 15-minute call this week?" — not three asks
  • Proof points: "We helped [similar company] reduce time-to-hire by 40%"
What Gets Ignored
  • Opening with "Hi, I'm [name] from Checkr…" — they don't care yet
  • Paragraphs longer than 3 lines
  • Listing all Checkr's products in email 1
  • Generic subject lines: "Quick question" or "Following up"
  • Attachments on a cold email
  • Ending with multiple asks or a long calendar link paragraph

Subject line guidance:

ContextStrong Subject Lines
Retail / High-volume hiring [Company] seasonal screening turnaround ✓ GOOD
Healthcare / Compliance License verification + adverse action — [Company] ✓ GOOD
Gig / Tech API reliability for [Company]'s onboarding flow ✓ GOOD
Any — pain framing Candidate drop-off at the screening step ✓ GOOD
Generic Quick question / Following up / Introduction ✗ AVOID
Product-first Checkr background screening for [Company] ✗ AVOID

Email template — first touch (retail example):

First Touch Email — Retail / High Volume Email · Day 1
Subject: [Company] seasonal screening turnaround Hi [First Name], Most retail HR teams I talk to lose candidates between offer and screening — usually because turnaround times from their current vendor stretch past what a seasonal hire will wait. Checkr has helped teams like [similar retailer] cut turnaround by 40% and reduce candidate drop-off at that step. Worth a 15-minute call to see if the same applies to [Company]? [Your name]
💡 Swap [similar retailer] for a real proof point from Checkpoint. Ask your manager for approved customer examples by industry.

LinkedIn connection note template:

LinkedIn Connection Request LinkedIn · Day 4
Hi [First Name] — I work with HR and TA teams at [industry] companies on their background screening operations. Happy to connect and share what I'm seeing others in your space doing differently.
💡 No pitch in the connection note. Wait for acceptance, then follow up with a question — not a pitch.
Cold Call Framework

The Cold Call Structure

The structure you certified on in Bootcamp — with full call guidance for live use.

StageWhat to Say / DoWhy It Works
Opening (0–10 sec)
Hook		 "Hey [name], this is [you] from Checkr — did I catch you at a bad time?" Gives them control. "Bad time" disarms defensiveness and usually gets a real answer.
Elevator Pitch (15–20 sec)
Value Bridge		 "We work with [industry] companies that are running background checks through [First Advantage / Sterling] and finding the turnaround times are hurting their ability to fill roles fast enough. That sound like anything you're dealing with?" Names a competitor (sets context), names a pain, ends with a question — not a pitch.
Discovery (if they engage)
Pain Surface		 "Tell me more about that — is it affecting your seasonal hiring or is it a year-round problem for you?" Deepens pain. Demonstrates industry knowledge. Keeps them talking.
Handle Objections
Trap Setting		 See Competitive Positioning section in Checkpoint for common objections by competitor (FA, Sterling, HireRight) Pre-set traps in bootcamp. Use them here.
CTA (close of call)
Next Step		 "I'd love to show you what a few of your peers in [industry] have done — do you have 20 minutes next [day] or [day] at [time]?" Offer two specific times, not an open-ended ask. Specific times close.
🎙
Voicemail Template
Under 20 seconds. Name, company, one sentence, callback twice.
"Hey [name], it's [your name] from Checkr — calling because we've been working with a few [industry] teams who were frustrated with their screening turnaround times. Worth a quick call — my number is [###-###-####], again that's [###-###-####]. Thanks."
Common Objections & Responses
  • "We're happy with our current vendor." → "That's actually the most common thing I hear before people realize what they're missing — what's your current turnaround on criminal?"
  • "Send me an email." → "Of course — can I ask one quick question first so I can make sure what I send is actually relevant to you?"
  • "We just renewed our contract." → "Totally understand — when does that expire? It takes us 90 days to stand up a new integration. Worth connecting 6 months before."
  • "Not the right person." → "Perfect — who should I be talking to about screening operations?" Get the name before you hang up.
Best Practices
  • Call between 8–10 AM and 4–5 PM in the prospect's timezone
  • Use direct dials from ZoomInfo — don't call the main line
  • Do not leave a voicemail on first 2 dials — call back at a different time
  • Log every call in Outreach (not Salesforce directly) — auto-syncs
  • After a booked meeting: send a calendar invite within 5 minutes
  • Gong records every call — your manager reviews these weekly

Calendar Blocking
Protect prospecting time before meetings fill your calendar
Block 9–11 AM every day as "Prospecting — Do Not Book." This is your outbound calling window. Block 4–5 PM as your second call block. Everything else — internal meetings, deal prep, admin — fills the gaps. If you don't block it, it will disappear.
