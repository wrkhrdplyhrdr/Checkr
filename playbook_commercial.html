Your Role

Commercial Sales Overview

What the Commercial AE role is, who you sell to, and what you're responsible for.

The Commercial Sales role at Checkr is focused on closing deals with companies between 500–5,000 employees, with deal sizes ranging from $5K to ~$250K. The goal is to move customers through the funnel as quickly as possible — under 30 days. Small, transactional deals can close in 1–2 calls. Larger, complex deals can take up to 60 days and may require a Solution Engineer.

Your role has two major parts: inbound/outbound opportunity management and revenue management. As a Commercial AE, you don't just sell the deals — you ensure your closed deals are running checks (bookings → revenue).

Working Inbound Leads

Inbound Lead Process

MQLs, SDR handoffs, and how to work leads that land in your queue.

Inbound MQLs associated with accounts in your territory are automatically routed to you in Salesforce. You have a 24-business-hour SLA to touch every high-intent MQL. Your first outreach is always a call — not an email.

Lead TypeHow You're NotifiedSLAFirst Action
High-intent MQL ("contact me" form) Email notification + My New MQLs in SFDC Checkr Sales Console 24 business hours Call first. Ready to qualify and hook. If no answer, add to best matching inbound sequence in Outreach.
Lower-intent lead (ebook, webinar) No email — visible in Action Required on SFDC home page As soon as possible Research with Sales Nav iframe in SFDC. Call first, then sequence if no answer.
SDR-qualified handoff SDR sends email intro to you and the prospect Review opp notes before first call Validate qualification info is in SFDC before calling. You own the relationship from here.
MQL Workflow — Step by Step
  • Check the campaign history field in SFDC — understand where they came from
  • Do 1 minute of research using the Sales Navigator iframe in SFDC
  • First outreach is always a phone call — call, qualify, hook them
  • If no answer, add to the matching Marketing Campaigns sequence in Outreach
  • Fallback: use the [Master] Inbound Lead Sequence if no campaign sequence fits
  • Move on — sequences handle follow-up, but only if you complete your tasks in Outreach
SDR → AE Handoff
  • SDR adds qualifying notes to the Stage 1 opp record before handoff
  • SDR moves opp to your name and associates the primary contact
  • Validate all qualification info is in the opp before your first call
  • SDR sends email intro — you are primary point of contact from that moment forward
  • Treat every SDR handoff as a warm lead — interest has already been validated
Self-Generated Pipeline

Outbound Motion

25% of your pipeline comes from you. ~200 target accounts assigned in SFDC. Work 3–5 contacts per account.

All outbound activity goes through Outreach — every contact should be in a sequence. Block a dedicated hour daily for prospecting. Minimum of 2 outbound SQOs per month. Best practice: aim high and exceed this consistently to keep pipeline healthy.

Account Research Order
  • 1. Demandbase — Intent score, MQA status, pages visited
  • 2. SCOUT (Credal) — Pain points, talking points, company context
  • 3. LinkedIn Sales Nav — Persona titles, org size, growth signals
  • 4. ZoomInfo — Direct dials, contact emails, org chart
  • 5. Salesforce — Any prior Checkr activity or history
Account Prioritization
  • Tier 1: Demandbase MQA — high intent, researching now
  • Tier 2: UserGems — former champion moved to a new company
  • Tier 3: Closed-lost re-engagement (confirm with manager first)
  • Tier 4: ICP-fit accounts with no prior Checkr history
  • Pro Tip: Target accounts with ATS tech stacks we integrate with — highest propensity to buy

Moving Deals Forward

The 6-Stage Sales Process

Each stage has strict entry and exit criteria. SFDC stage must be kept current — this is how you forecast and how leadership tracks pipeline health.

For Commercial deals you have three core calls: Discovery, Demo, and Pricing Proposal. You may combine all three into one call for smaller, transactional deals. Your goal is always to move customers through as fast as possible while delivering a great experience.

1
Forecast: Omitted
Interest
Discovery call scheduled. At least one contact role associated with the opp. Interest opps are NOT counted in pipeline. Must be updated within 24 hours of the disco call.
Exit Criteria
Discovery call completed, pain validated, opp updated with notes
2
Forecast: Pipeline (~35% close)
Discovery
Validate pains Checkr can support. Identify current state, negative consequences, future state, positive outcomes, compelling event. Understand tech stack and competitive landscape.
Exit Criteria
Pain confirmed, tech stack understood, MEDDPICC fields populated, next step set
3
Forecast: Pipeline
Establish Requirements
Uncover required capabilities and map to positive business outcomes. Share how Checkr does it better. Engage SE for solutioning if needed. Start ironing out pricing and packaging. ID Economic Buyer.
Exit Criteria
Champion confirmed, access to Economic Buyer established
4
Forecast: Best Case (60% close)
Propose / Evaluate
General need confirmed but hurdles remain (product, pricing, timing). Proposal delivered, evaluation underway. There's a real deal but it's not locked yet.
Exit Criteria
Proposal accepted in principle, pricing validated, mutual close plan agreed
5
Forecast: Commit (90%+ close)
Negotiate / Close
MSA/legal in flight or complete. Pricing agreed. Deal closing barring unforeseen blockers. Close date must be accurate. Next Steps must be dated with your last action and next action.
Exit Criteria
MSA signed, order form executed, kickoff scheduled
6
Closed Won → Revenue Management
Revenue Management
Deal closed. Now ensure the customer runs checks. Complete all post-close activities. Facilitate handoff to CS/Implementation. Monitor consumption. Manage upsell per ROE.
Your Responsibility
Bookings → Revenue. You own revenue management until CS takes over at 3 months post-sale.
📝
Next Steps Field — Non-Negotiable
Always date your Next Steps entry
Format: "11/3 — Sent redlined paperwork to client. Will follow up Friday if no response." Every active opp must have a dated next step at all times. This is what powers accurate forecasting.
Forecasting

Forecasting Your Business

A forecast is an expression of expected revenue. The best forecasts are accurate within ±10%. You're expected to forecast weekly.

Thursday AMAEs + 1st Line ManagersConfirm SFDC fields updated, weekly forecast tracker reviewed
Thursday PM1st Line Managers + RevOps DirManagers present forecast for RevOps and leadership inspection

Forecast Checklist — Ask Yourself
  • Are all Quotes correct? Price, eACV, Contract Type?
  • Did I have a relevant meeting for each committed opp this week?
  • Is the Close Date accurate — or am I wishful thinking?
  • Are Next Steps updated with a date and a specific action?
  • Is Forecast Category consistent with the Stage?
Volume Pressure-Test Questions
  • "What is driving your requirement — compliance or staffing?"
  • "Your check volume in [year] looked like X — is that typical?"
  • "What growth does your company have on the horizon this year?"
  • "Is your hiring focus on growing headcount or backfilling positions?"
Rules of Engagement

Account & Opportunity ROE

Know whether you're working a Prospect or an Existing Customer before touching any account. When in doubt, ask your manager before starting any sales activity.

ScenarioWho Owns ItWhat You Need to Know
New prospect — no prior Checkr historyYou (Commercial AE)Create opp in SFDC before any sales activity begins
Account you closed — 0–3 months post-saleYou (with conditions)New opp must be created within 3 business days of prior MSA signature, fully documented, and CS must be aware and in agreement
Account you closed — 3+ months post-saleCustomer Success (AAM)AE phased out. Open opps transfer to CS unless CBO or SVP CS grants an exception
Inbound from a current customerCS / AAM teamAlways routed to CS — staffed or unstaffed. If you close a misrouted upsell it will not count as commissionable bookings.
AE departure before 3-month windowSegment Leader determinesOpen opps transferred to Segment Leader — may continue in sales or transfer to CS early
⚠️
When ROE is Broken
Broken ROE — regardless of intent — puts payment at risk
It is your responsibility to know whether you are working with a Prospect or an Existing Customer. SFDC is the source of record. When in doubt, ask your manager, Sr. Director, or RevOps partner before touching the account. Never assume.
